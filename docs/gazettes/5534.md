## Notes on costs of 2 Meg uplink/downlink:

**Asymmetric**: With the Asymmetric service, the data speed or amount of transmitted data differs in one direction compared with the other direction. The system uses most of the channel to transmit downstream to the user and only a small part to receive information from the user. Meaning, downlink and uplink speeds are for instance on a 1:2 or 1:3 ratio (Example 1024k downlink and 512k Uplink depending on how it is technically defined and provisioned).

**Symmetric**: With the symmetric service, the data speed or amount of transmitted data is the same in both directions. The system uses the channel to transmit downstream to the user at the same rate or amount of transmitted data as to receiving information from the user. Uplink and downlink speeds are on a ratio of 1:1 (Example 1024k downlink and 1024k uplink).

### REASONS FOR THE DECISION ARE AS FOLLOWS:

Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre submitted applications for a community broadcasting service licence and spectrum use licence on 12 June 2013 for consideration by the Authority, in accordance with sections 85 and 101 of the Communications Act, 2009 (Act No. 8 of 2009) and the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences and Spectrum Use Licences as published in Government Gazette No. 4785, General Notice No. 272 of 29 August 2011.

As per the constitution submitted, Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre is a community project, founded in 2012 to provide community broadcasting services in Khorixas and surrounding areas and intends to broadcast under the K-FM brand name. An application for funding and training of local Namibians was submitted to the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organisation (UNESCO) Windhoek office.

In terms of section 101(6) of the Communications Act, 2009 (Act No. 8 of 2009) a community broadcasting service licence application must be accompanied by a spectrum use licence application if the applicant intends to use spectrum in the provision of the intended broadcasting service. Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre submitted an application for spectrum use for FM broadcasting frequency between 87 MHz and 108 MHz dated 13 June 2013 in the geographical area of Khorixas and a transmitter output power of 600 Watt. Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre intends to construct their own broadcasting transmitter tower and provide their own signal distribution service as required by regulation 5(2) of the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences and Spectrum Use Licences.

Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre submitted a program schedule, constitution and certified copies of identity documents as required on the application forms for service and spectrum use on 23 August 2013. The program schedule stipulates that FM radio services will be broadcasted from 06h00 to 22h00 from Monday to Sunday. The spectrum use application form stated that the broadcasting transmitter tower will be located at House No. D1, Khorixas.

An applicant for a community service licence is required to submit financial resources available as required by Regulation 5 (2)(k) of the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences and Spectrum Use Licences -

"include a statement of accounts setting out the financial resources available to the applicant in relation to the broadcasting service applied for;"

Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre submitted the authorization letter to construct a broadcasting transmitter tower by Khorixas Town Council on 24 April 2014. UNESCO confirmed funding of the project as well as a detailed outline of implementation and training of staff and volunteers from the community directly to the Authority on 19 May 2014.

Following due process in terms of regulation 11 of the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences and Spectrum Use Licences, the Authority published a notice in the Government Gazette 5374, Notice No. 520, dated 24 December 2013, allowing fourteen (14) days for public comments from the date of publication of the Notice in the Gazette. The comments period closed on the 6th of January 2014 and no comments were received and subsequently no reply comments were requested from Khorixas Youth and Community Media Centre. The last day for a decision is the 18th of July 2014.

The Kunene region is home to an estimated population of 68,735 as reflected in the Namibia 2011 Population and Housing Census Main Report (Namibian Statistics Agency, 2013). Khorixas is surrounded by approximately 50 villages and has limited access to information and broadcasting services.
